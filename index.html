Our Specials

Crispy Honey Garlic Chicken

Honey garlic chicken is a delicious and popular dish that combines the sweetness of honey with the savory flavors of garlic and chicken. It's a versatile recipe that can be prepared in various ways, such as baked, grilled, or stir-fried.

Red Hot Buffalo Chicken

Our Story

Filipino cuisine is a vibrant and diverse culinary tradition that reflects the rich history, cultural influences, and local ingredients of the Philippines. It is characterized by a unique blend of flavors, colors, and textures that make it a delightful and memorable experience.
Filipino food often showcases a combination of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. It incorporates a wide variety of ingredients, including rice, meats, seafood, vegetables, tropical fruits, and aromatic spices. Here are a few popular dishes from Filipino cuisine.

Menu

Breakfast & Starters

Tapsilog
Tapsilog is the OG when it comes to Filipino silog. It consists of beef tapa that’s been pan-fried and served with garlic rice and fried eggs.
Longsilog
longsilog is the best. It consists of Filipino longganisa sausages served with garlic fried rice and eggs.
Tocilog
It’s made with Filipino tocino, which is sweetened and cured pork belly.
Spamsilog
As you can probably guess from its name, spamsilog is a type of silog made with every Filipino’s favorite brand of luncheon meat – SPAM

Popular Dishes

Chicken Adobo
The most popular Filipino food and referred to as the unofficial national dish of the Philippines, Adobo is commonly chicken (though pork is a 2nd favourite option) simmered in vinegar, garlic, black peppercorns, soy sauce, and bay leaves.
Sinigang
Sinigang is one of the best Filipino foods to try if you’re searching for something that might warm you up during the monsoon season. This traditional Filipino food is meat or seafood-based Filipino stew or soup in which the acidic stock is made with tamarind (sampalok) for a sour and savoury taste.
Lechon
The most iconic Filipino food, Lechon, often known as “roasted suckling pig,” is a famous Filipino dish served at festivities and special occasions such as birthdays and weddings.
Bicol Express
Bicol Express is a spicy Filipino stew that contains bite-sized pork pieces with chilies, coconut milk, shrimp paste, tomatoes, onions, and garlic.

Dinner

Filipino Escabeche
This appetizing recipe is known for its sweet and sour qualities as a result of a glorious mix of seasonings and other ingredients. Escabeche, in most of its forms, makes use of vinegar for that classic sour taste in our sauce, which we use to coat either fish or meat
Pinanyihang Manok
Pininyahang Manok, as the name implies, relies largely on the strong flavor of our yellow fruit to enrich a yummy chicken dish.
Pancit
Throw together some sotanghon and canton noodles alongside a mix of different proteins equally tasty. Add your vegetables, sauces, and more, and you have the fantastic Pancit
Chicken Inasal
Inasal is a Filipino dish wherein chicken pieces are marinated in a special blend of ingredients and grilled or roasted until perfectly cooked.

Desserts

Leche Flan
Leche Flan is a dessert made-up of eggs and milk with a soft caramel on top. It resembles crème caramel and caramel custard.
Macaroni Fruit Salad
Macaroni Fruit Salad is a favorite Filipino dessert during the holidays and special occasions. This is not your typical macaroni salad. It is more of a dessert rather than a side dish. It is sweet, creamy, and heavenly.
Binignit
Binignit is a type of fruit and tubers stew cooked in coconut milk. This Filipino meryenda dish originated from the Visayas region in the Philippines. It is mostly similar to Ginataang Halo-halo,except that the latter uses glutinous rice balls or bilo-bilo as part of the ingredient.
Buko Pie
One of the all-time favorite “pasalubong” is the Buko pie. This sweet and rich pie is made from young coconut meat which is locally known as “Buko” meat. “Pasalubong”, on the other hand, is a gift for a family member or friend bough by a traveler returning from a trip.
